Early Space Exploration

Early space exploration dates back to the mid-20th century when both the United States and the Soviet Union launched their first satellites. The Soviet Union achieved a historic milestone with the launch of Sputnik 1 in 1957, marking the beginning of the space age.

The Space Race

The Space Race was a period of intense competition between the United States and the Soviet Union to achieve various milestones in space exploration. The race culminated with the U.S. Apollo 11 mission in 1969, when Neil Armstrong became the first human to set foot on the Moon.

Exploration Beyond Earth's Orbit

Exploration beyond Earth's orbit continued with missions to the Moon, Mars, and other celestial bodies. Notable missions include the Mars rovers and the International Space Station (ISS), which serves as a platform for international cooperation in space exploration.
