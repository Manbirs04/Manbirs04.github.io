Space Exploration Encyclopedia

Yuri Gagarin

Yuri Gagarin was a Soviet astronaut and the first human to travel into space. On April 12, 1961, he piloted the Vostok 1 spacecraft, completing one orbit around the Earth. His achievement made him an international hero.

Neil Armstrong

Neil Armstrong made history as the commander of the Apollo 11 mission, during which he became the first person to set foot on the Moon on July 20, 1969. His iconic words, "That's one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind," are etched in history.

Valentina Tereshkova

Valentina Tereshkova, a Soviet cosmonaut, became the first woman to travel into space in 1963. She orbited the Earth aboard Vostok 6, paving the way for more women to participate in space exploration.
